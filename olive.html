Olive Health App

My Intent

Olive is a health app centered around organizing personal health information, aiming to assist those interested in exercise and fitness, dietary health, and medication tracking. The app is designed for the general public who want to be mindful of personal health amidst busy daily life and could benefit from the provided structure.

Initial Research

Research goals:

  • how the average person views health apps
  • tools that participants deem helpful and may use

What I hoped to learn:

From having open conversations with participants, I planned to learn more about health application perceptions, daily habits, and how my health app can be incorporated with these habits, and any other opinions or perspectives about health tools.

Based on interviews done, potential users are looking for convenient and accurate statistics trackers, variety in categories and topics to organize and learn about, and more detailed and customizable medication tools.

Sitemap

The combination of interviews and persona-building provided a clearer picture and helped me to create a sitemap, which became the blueprint for Olive and all its features. Planning out the potential journey and steps that users may take presented tasks and goals Olive can assist with. This helped form categories that developed into the pages seen in the above sitemap.

Card Sorting

Open card sorting results yielded patterns and helped me refine the structure of pages and content within Olive. Grouping content that was recognized together will help users make these connections, leading to smoother navigating and helping to complete tasks within Olive more easily.

Wireframing

Early in the design phase, I started working on 3 main challenges, reflected by the target audience:

  • health information offered as a learning resource
  • health stats tracking
  • being able to set reminders
Sketching on pen and paper to designing higher fidelity prototypes helped me stay focused on users' needs.

Moderated Testing

With an interactive, high-fidelity version of Olive, I then conducted moderated usability tests with real users!

Objectives:

  • Learn if participants are able to grasp app’s tools without a detailed onboarding tutorial
  • Determine if participants understand the search section’s function/purpose (for finding health-related articles)
  • Observe how participants interact with data tracking system and if reactions are in favor of said structure
  • Receive feedback on accessibility options, if expansive enough or too expansive

View Test Plan

User Testing

Outcome

This usability test allowed me to observe how my participants interacted with my prototype to try to complete the tasks I gave. The focus was on the 3 challenges I was interested in learning more about, which were setting reminders, searching for information resources, and accessibility settings. Each one led to useful feedback on these specific features but also allowed me to gather feedback about the general functionality of the app’s layout.

To analyze the usability test results, I used affinity mapping and a rainbow spreadsheet to help me organize and prioritize the observed issues. Picking out and grouping significant feedback helped visualize problems and positives.

I found that there were a few issues needed to be resolved:

  • unclear onboarding tutorial
  • users were unsure of the purpose for certain features (e.g. Search section, app settings options, alarm icons)
  • visibility problems like button sizes being too small

Final Thoughts

As proud as I am with the design so far, I know that there’s always areas in which I can improve. What I noticed is that it’s hard to balance customization and usability. It’s a challenge to draw focus on one without the sacrifice of the other. I’m excited to work on more projects and studies that will help me have a better understanding of how to balance the two and at times know when it’s right to maybe even prioritize one over the other. With Olive, I had the opportunity to go through the whole process of a user experience design and receive feedback from peers, teachers, and everyday folks. It’s been eye-opening learning how to communicate and convey my ideas to different people, as I’m sure it will be important in my uxer journey and the rest of my career.
